Mutton Bunny Chow

Ingredients

  • 1kg bone-in mutton
  • 2 large diced tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • 2 sliced onions
  • 2tbsp crushed garlic-ginger mix
  • Coriander leaves
  • 3-5 green chilis
  • Quarter pieces of unsliced white bread loaf
  • Whole spice
    • 1 large cinnomon stick
    • 2 bay leaves
    • 3 cardomon pods
    • 1 star anise
  • Crushed spice
    • 1-2tbs cumin-coriander
    • 2-3tbs red curry spice mix
    • 1tbs garam masala
    • 1tsp tumeric

Steps

  1. Add whole spices to about half a cup of oil in a hot pan.
  2. After whole spices become aromatic, toss in the onions.
  3. Fry the onions for about 10-15 minutes, getting char on the edges.
  4. Put the mutton in to get some colour, but we won't try for a deep brown
  5. Once the raw looking of the mutton is gone, and we see smidgens of brown, add garlic-ginger
  6. Work quickly now, as soon as the garlic-ginger smells cooked, add all spices but reserve half the garam
  7. The spices need to be well-cooked but not burnt, about 1-5 minutes depending on how much oil and water is in the pan
  8. Now add tomatoes, tomato paste, and half the green chilis and stir
  9. Add salt and about 500ml-1000ml water, and simmer for 2-3 hours until meat is soft and gravy is thick but not dry
  10. Drop in the rest of the green chilis, the garam, and coriander leaves and taste for salt
  11. Use a quarter loaf of unsliced bread as a bowl, using the taking the fluffy white inside out, using that part to dab the gravy
